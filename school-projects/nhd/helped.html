How It Helped

A Summary From history.state.gov

Pres. Truman said he would provide assistance to all democratic countries in danger of communism.
It changed our foreign policy to become more involved with conflicts that don't affect the US.
The cause for the speech was an announcement from the British Government that said they would no longer assist Greece or Turkey.
Truman addressed Greece and Turkey. He asked congress for $400 million, military personnel, and equipment. Truman argued that a Communist victory would make the Middle East politically unstable. It could also threaten US national security.

The Marshall Plan

The Aftermath of WWII caused much of Europe to be in ruin. The US thought that the state Europe was in could cause a change to communism. Europe was hungry and devastated and needed assistance.
The Marshall Plan, much like the Truman Doctrine, was another program to provide countries with aid. $13 billion was given to Europe to reconstruct the economy and infrastructure.
It was very successful in helping Europe and boosted the American economy as well.

Problems in Greece and Turkey

Turkey--

  • Turkish Straits (image at right) - Soviets pressured Turkey to allow free shipping through the Black Sea Straits. Tensions arose when the Turkish government denied them passage.
  • British Aid End - British assistance to Turkey ended in 1947. Turkey needed support to keep control of the Straits.

    • Greece--

  • EAM rally - National Liberation Front of the Greek Communists (KKE). A shooting at an EAM rally by the British in Athens resulted in civil war.
  • Josip Tito was directly funding the KKE. No help was given by the Soviets.
