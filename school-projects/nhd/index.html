The Truman Doctrine

During the Cold War, the Soviet Union influenced the growth of many countries. Greece and Turkey in particular were having issues with rebellions against their democratic governments. The Soviet Union was partially if not fully responsible for supporting these conflicts.
America at the time had a very strict foreign policy on aiding other countries with their struggles. President Harry S. Truman believed that there was a high risk of many countries converting to communism. He needed to amend the foreign policy so the US could assist countries under this threat. President Truman presented the Truman Doctrine to congress, a compromise that would allow the US to help other countries.
