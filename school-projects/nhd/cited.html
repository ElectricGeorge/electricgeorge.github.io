Annotated Bibliography

A+E Networks. "Truman Doctrine Is Announced." History.com. Last modified 2009. Accessed December 4, 2017. http://www.history.com/this-day-in-history/truman-doctrine-is-announced.
This short article gives information on what Pres. Truman said in his speech. It summarizes it with important details. I used it to find general speech information. It is a secondary source from history.com. 

Bulletin of the American Council for a Democratic Greece (New York, NY). "Record of Greek Government." January 1947, volume IV, No. 1 edition. Accessed March 14, 2018. https://www.marxists.org/subject/greek-civil-war/bulletin-american-council/1947/01/record-government.htm.
This article gives information on what the Greek Government was like during 1947. It gives a record of critical government attributes. It is a primary source from an American bulletin specifically for Greece. 

Bureau of Public Affairs. "The Truman Doctrine, 1947." Office of the Historian. Last modified 2016. Accessed December 4, 2017. https://history.state.gov/milestones/1945-1952/truman-doctrine.
I used this to find information on why the Truman Doctrine was decided. It gives the backstory on the Soviets meddling with Greece and Turkey. It is a Truman Doctrine summary (secondary source) from a US government site.

Eliot, George Fielding, Major. "What the Truman Doctrine Really Means." See Magazine, 1947, 1-2. Accessed March 14, 2018. http://www.oldmagazinearticles.com/article-summary/harry_s_truman_cold_warrior.
A magazine's explanation/opinion of the Truman Doctrine. It is a primary source which gives me information on what people thought of the Truman Doctrine at the time. It is an American magazine that gives information on wars and why this change helped.

Greek Boston. "Truman Doctrine Aids Greece Against Communism." Greek Boston. Accessed March 12, 2018. http://www.greekboston.com/culture/modern-history/truman-doctrine-communism/.
This article has info on how Greece benefitted from the doctrine. They argue that the Communist Party probably would have won the Greek Civil War if it wasn't for US aid. This is a secondary source from a greek website.

Greek-Turkish Aid Act, Pub. L. No. 80-75, 61 Stat. 4 (May 22, 1947). Accessed March 14, 2018. http://www.legisworks.org/congress/80/publaw-75.pdf.
This is the actual Truman Doctrine law. I will use it to find information about the actual act that isn't summarized in articles. This is a US Statute, so it is a primary source.

Hinton, Harold B. "Truman Signs Bill for Near East Aid as 'Step to Peace.'" The New York Times, May 22, 1947. Accessed March 14, 2018. https://archive.nytimes.com/www.nytimes.com/learning/general/onthisday/big/0522.html.
It gives information that was written at the time of the Truman Doctrine. It is valuable to see what newspapers wrote about at the time and how well people supported it. This is a primary source.

Hogan, Michael J. A Cross of Iron: Harry S. Truman and the Origins of the National Security State, 1945-1954. Transferred to digital print. ed. Cambridge: Cambridge Univ. Press, 2007. PDF e-book.
This book gives information on National Security and US intervention. It provides additional information about the Truman Doctrine's background and why it was made. It is a secondary source book.

Jones, Howard. A New Kind of War: America's Global Strategy and the Truman Doctrine in Greece. Oxford: Oxford University Press, USA, 1989. Accessed March 15, 2018. https://books.google.com/books?isbn=019535429X.
A book about US's global intervention. I'll get more information about our foreign policies from here. Unfortunately, I can only view part of it.

McGhee, Geoge. The US-Turkish-NATO Middle East Connection: How the Truman Doctrine and Turkey's NATO Entry Contained the Soviets. Springer, 2016. https://books.google.com/books?isbn=1349205036.
A book that gives information on Turkey and the Cold War. I use it for additional notes. Unfortunately, this is a preview.

"Start of the Cold War - The Truman Doctrine and the Marshall Plan." Khan Academy. Accessed December 4, 2017. https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities/ap-us-history/period-8/apush-postwar-era/a/start-of-the-cold-war-part-2.
This article contains info about the Marshall Plan, another way the US aided countries under potential threat of communism. It is a secondary source from an educational organization.

Truman, Harry. "Truman Doctrine." Speech, March 12, 1947. Video file, 02:00. History.com. Accessed March 14, 2018. https://www.history.com/speeches/the-truman-doctrine.
The speech given to congress by Pres. Truman known as the Truman Doctrine announcement. I used this to pull information from the speech itself. It is a primary source.

Wikimedia Foundation. "Greek Civil War." Wikipedia. Last modified March 5, 2018. Accessed March 14, 2018. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Civil_War.
This source gives info on the Greek Civil War. I used it to find out what it was like in Greece before and after the Truman Doctrine. This is a secondary source.

———. "Truman Doctrine." Wikipedia. Last modified November 30, 2017. Accessed December 1, 2017. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truman_Doctrine.
This will be used for finding general information about the Truman Doctrine. It gives information on the problems in Greece and Turkey and Truman's speech. It is a secondary source.

———. "Turkish Straits Crisis." Wikipedia. Last modified February 27, 2018. Accessed March 14, 2018. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Straits_crisis.
This is for finding more information on why the US wanted to aid Turkey specifically. The Turkish Straits crisis was a deciding factor of the Truman Doctrine. This is a secondary source.
